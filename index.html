Fertility Assessment
(Subfertility & Infertility)

"Infertility is a condition of the reproductive system defined by the failure to achieve a clinical pregnancy after 12 months or more of regular unprotected sexual intercourse." – World Health Organization

Definitions & Epidemiology

Infertility – failure to conceive after 12 months of regular unprotected intercourse (or 6 months if the woman is ≥35 years). Affects approximately 8–12% of couples worldwide.

Reference: ASRM 2025 Guidelines, NICE CG156

Causes of Subfertility

  • ⚡ Female factors: ~30% (ovulation disorders, tubal disease, uterine anomalies)
  • 🧬 Male factors: ~30% (spermatogenesis failure, ductal obstruction, impaired sperm quality)
  • 🤝 Combined/mixed: ~30%
  • ❓ Unexplained: ~10–20% despite standard workup

Source: Hong Kong FHS 2016; Murtagh 2025

Age & Fertility Decline

  • After 32 years → female fertility declines by 1.5% per year
  • Pregnancy rate after 12 months: <31y → 74%; 31–35y → 62%; >35y → 54%
  • Male fertility → subtle decline after ~40–45 years (sperm quality, DNA fragmentation)

Source: ASRM 2025, ObG Project 2025

Referral timeline

<35 years: 12 months
≥35 years: 6 months

Both partners

Always assess together

Unexplained

~10–20% of couples

Female Factor Assessment

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF)

  • Indications: tubal factor, severe male factor, unexplained after IUI failure, endometriosis
  • Live birth rate per cycle ~30–40% for women <35y
  • Preimplantation genetic testing (PGT) available for monogenic/chromosomal disorders

Third‑party reproduction

  • Donor sperm (intracervical or IUI)
  • Donor oocytes / embryos
  • Surrogacy (where legal)

PHN Role & Nursing Care

  • Health education & prevention: Promote optimal weight, smoking cessation, limit alcohol/caffeine, manage chronic conditions (diabetes, thyroid).
  • Assessment & early referral: Obtain detailed reproductive history, identify red flags (irregular cycles, endometriosis, prior pelvic infection, varicocele).
  • Psychological support: Subfertility causes significant distress – provide empathetic, non‑judgmental counselling, refer to support groups.
  • Coordination of care: Navigate diagnostic workup, assist with insurance/treatment coverage, coordinate with REI specialists.
  • Lifestyle interventions: Weight loss (5–10% of body weight improves ovulation), moderate exercise, stress reduction techniques.

DOH / Public Health implication:

Subfertility prevention is integrated into family planning, STI/HIV prevention, and preconception care programs.
